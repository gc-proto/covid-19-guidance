What expenses can be claimed

Simplified option: Daily-rate claim

No reciepts needed No work space calculation needed No T2200 form needed

To simplify your claim for work space expenses and office supply, internet, and phone expenses due to the COVID-19 pandemic, you can claim $2 per day you worked from home (to a maximum of $400). This option is for your 2020 tax return.

If you are claiming the actual costs you incurred and not using the simplified option, you must determine the amount of home expenses you can claim for your work space. You must separate the expenses between your employment use and non-employment (personal) use of your home.

Home expenses

If you meet the eligibility criteria, you can claim a portion of certain home expenses that support your work space in the home.

Commission employees, who typically have an amount in box 42 on their T4 slip, can claim some expenses that salaried employees cannot.

Can be claimed

All salaried employees and commission employees can claim

Household expenses:

  • maintenance and minor repair costs Footnote 1
  • electricity
  • heat
  • water
  • utility (electricity, heat, and water) portion of condominium fees
  • rent Footnote 2

If used in the work space:

  • cleaning supplies
  • wall paint
  • light bulbs

Commission employees can also claim

  • property taxes
  • home insurance

Can't be claimed

Salaried employees and commission employees cannot claim

  • mortgage interest
  • mortgage payments
  • furniture
  • wall decorations
  • capital cost allowance (the tax deduction for items that depreciate, such as furniture and equipment)
  • capital expenses (replacing windows, flooring, furnace etc)Footnote 3

Office supplies, internet, and phone expenses

If you employer requires you to pay for office supplies, an internet connection, or a phone, you may be able to claim those expenses.

Although you can claim these expenses, they are not related to the physical work space in your home. They are claimed on a different section of the T777 (Short) or T777 form.

Office supplies
What can be claimed
It is an item that is used up while directly performing your job
How much to claim
You can claim only the expense, or portion of the expense, you use for work

Limitations on expenses

The employment expenses you can claim are limited when:

  • you work only a portion of the year from your home:
    • You can claim the expenses you paid in the portion of the year you worked from home. You cannot claim the expenses you paid for the whole year.
  • you have multiple income sources:
    • You can deduct work space expenses only from the income the expenses relate to, and not from any other income.
  • your expenses exceed your income:
    • The amount you can deduct for work space expenses is limited to the amount of employment income that is left after you have deducted all other employment expenses. This means that you cannot use work space expenses to create or increase a loss from employment. If you cannot deduct all your work space expenses in the year, you can carry forward the expenses. You can deduct these expenses in the next year as long as you are reporting income from the same employer. However, you cannot create or increase a loss from employment by carrying forward work space expenses.
    • If you are a commission employee, you may not be able to claim your total commission expenses if they exceed your commission income. To learn more, refer to: T4404 - Chapter 2 - Employees earning commission income
Footnote 1

Maintenance and minor repair costs
The maintenance and minor repair costs generally fall into one of the following categories:

  • Expenses you paid that relate to the work space as well as other areas of the home. You can claim the percentage of those expenses that relate to the work space.
    • Example: Minor repairs of the home furnace or air-conditioner or the purchase of household cleaning products.
  • Expenses related to the work space only. You can deduct the total amount of the expenses if the amount paid is reasonable.
    • Example: Purchase of light bulbs, repainting the work space, or repairing walls or ceilings after the installation of phones, fax machines or other office equipment you used in the work space.
  • Expenses related to a part of the house that you did not use as a work space. You cannot claim any part of those expenses.
    • Example: Repainting a bedroom.

Rent

  • If you rent your home, you can claim a reasonable portion of the rent related to the work space.
  • If you own your home, you cannot claim the rental value of the work space in your home.

Capital expenses

  • The renovations and expenses that extend the useful life of a property or improve it beyond its original condition usually are capital expenses.
    • Example: Changing a furnace, changing a window or improving the flooring of a room.
